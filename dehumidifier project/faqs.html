Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Find answers to some of the most common questions we receive. If you need further assistance, feel free to contact us.

A dehumidifier is a device that reduces the level of humidity in the air. It works by drawing in the moist air, cooling it to condense the water vapor, and then releasing the dry air back into the room.

We recommend cleaning your dehumidifier every two weeks, especially the filter and water reservoir. This ensures optimal performance and longevity of your device.

Yes, dehumidifiers can be used in any room, but different models are designed for specific conditions. For instance, a portable dehumidifier is great for small spaces, while a whole-house dehumidifier is ideal for large areas with high humidity levels.

The cost to run a dehumidifier varies depending on its size and energy efficiency. On average, a dehumidifier consumes between 250 to 500 watts of electricity, which costs around $0.03 to $0.10 per hour to operate, depending on local electricity rates.
